What causes us to sleep? The interplay of two biological processes!

Process-S:

This is your sleep drive. It increases the longer you don’t sleep and decreases once you do.

Process-C:

This is your body's natural clock, the circadian rhythm. It oscillates between night and day. Whether you actually sleep or not doesn’t matter

So when do we sleep?

When the distance between the two curves becomes too great.

Why should we sleep?

Sleep has manyfold benefits, both for your physical and mental health

Physical health benefits:

  • Strengthens the immune system
  • Reduce risk of heart disease and strokes
  • Reduce risk of cancer
  • Increases chance of successful dieting
  • Improves performance in sports and recovery

      How Long Did You Sleep?

      Caffeine

      Caffeine helps your concentration and even increases your body's tolerance for pain. However it makes sleeping almost impossible. With a half-life-time of 3 to 5 hours caffeine stays in your body and keeps you from getting good sleep for far longer than you might expect. Avoid consuming caffeine after noon.

      Establish a bedtime routine

      What you do in the hour before bed has an enormous impact on the quality of your sleep.
      Watching TV and being on your computer or phone is not a good idea. They are extremely stimulating for the brain and by that prevent winding down. They also emit blue light which puts your brain in “awake-mode”. Quiet activities like reading or meditation are great for getting your mind ready for sleep.
      Doing sports is a great way to exhaust yourself and clear your head before bed. However if done just before going to bed, the high body temperature will make falling asleep harder.
